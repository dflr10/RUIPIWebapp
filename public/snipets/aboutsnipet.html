About RUIPI

The Unique Registry for the Identification of Indigenous Patients RUIPI is a project developed using the Java programming language, in conjunction with the MySQL database manager and Digita Persona fingerprint reader for the identification of Indigenous patients belonging to the Wiwa, Kogui and Arahuaca communities.

How RUIPI is conceived?

This project was born at the beginning of 2020, when the students of the training program in analysis and development of information systems of the Sena espv and dflr after carrying out a scan of information and a diagnosis for the creation of a software tailored to the problems found in the IPS Gonnawindua Ette Ennaka.

Why RUIPI?

The Unique Registry for the Identification of Indigenous Patients is created in response to the problem of the lack of a characterization of indigenous communities, specifically the Kogui, Wiwa and Arahuaca communities, who, by different interpretations, conceptions and community and identity perspectives, are reticent to the concept of identification under which the Colombian state is protected. In addition to this contingency of a sociicultural type there is the problem of the coverage that indigenous populations have regarding access to essential goods, among which is the registration of their persons in the registry offices and the scant work carried out by government entities to counteract the existing gap. For this reason, RUIPI seeks, without wanting to replace the identity document issued by the registry solve in an efficient way the problem of lack of characterization. Using the use of biometric recognition technologies, and with the implementation of a software that stores and allows the modification of user and patient profiles, as well as the access and modification of the clinical history and the visualization of the programs prevention and early detection of diseases, RUIPI seeks to improve the processes related to with patient care, as well as medical experience with them.
